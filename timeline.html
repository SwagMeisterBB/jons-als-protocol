My ALS Journey

From the first sign something was wrong to where I am today.

OCT
2019
October 2019

Something Was Wrong

On a hunting trip, I kept tripping and falling. Over and over. I knew something was not right.

DEC
2019
December 2019

Surgery in Colorado Springs

Diagnosed with collapsed L5 and S1. Had surgery to repair them. Started physical therapy.

2020
2021
2020 - 2021

Two Years of Searching

9 specialists. 12 MRIs. 3 brain scans. Blood panels. CAT scans. X-rays. A spinal tap. Nobody could tell me what was wrong.

SEP
2021
September 2021

Diagnosed with ALS

At Anschutz Hospital in Aurora, Colorado: "I am 90% certain you have ALS." They gave me 2 to 5 years to live. Only 5% of ALS patients live longer than 10 years. I decided right then that I would be in that 5%.

LATE
2021
Late 2021

Entered the Courage Clinical Trial

I followed their plan. Research trials, physical therapy, prescribed drugs. Did everything they told me.

LATE
2022
Late 2022

The Call: Stop. It Doesn't Work.

The trial ended. I was in steep decline. Slurred speech, weak legs, using a walker. Their way was not working.

EARLY
2023
Early 2023

I Decided to Do It My Way

Started researching supplements obsessively. Began weight training against doctors' advice. Could barely leg press 50 pounds.

MID
2023
Mid 2023

Built My Protocol

23 supplements, organized by category. Exercise grew to 1.5 to 2 hours, 5 to 6 days a week. Monitoring every change.

2024
2024

The Improvements Were Real

Ankles stable. Knees stable on stairs. Speech improving. Leg pressing 200 pounds for 4 sets of 15. Doctors are impressed.

2025
2025

Started Sharing My Protocol

Phone calls with other ALS patients became routine. Connected with the Rocky Mountain ALS community.

MAR
2026
March 2026

I'M NOT DYING TODAY

This site launches. 23 supplements documented. Everything I know, organized for anyone who needs it.
