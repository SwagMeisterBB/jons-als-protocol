My ALS Journey: 6+ Years and Counting

This is the real timeline. From the first sign something was wrong to right now. Every turning point, every decision, every time I chose to keep fighting.

Oct 2019

The hunting trip

I kept tripping and falling. Over and over. I'm an outdoorsman. I know how to walk through rough terrain. Something was wrong and I knew it.

Dec 2019

Back surgery in Colorado Springs

Collapsed L5 and S1. They thought that was the problem. It wasn't. But we didn't know that yet.

2020-2021

Two years of searching

9 specialists. 12 MRIs. 3 brain scans. Full blood panels. A spinal tap. Two years of "we don't know what's wrong with you." Nobody had answers.

Sep 2021

Diagnosed with ALS

Anschutz Hospital, Aurora, Colorado. "You have 2 to 5 years." I sat there with my wife and heard those words. Right then I decided I would be in the 5%.

Late 2021

Entered the Courage ALS clinical trial

I followed their plan. Did what they told me. Took what they gave me. I trusted the process because I didn't know any better yet.

Late 2022

Trial ended. Steep decline.

Got the phone call: "Stop taking it, it doesn't work." I was in steep decline. Using a walker. My speech was slurred. That was the lowest point.

Early 2023

Decided to do it my way

I started researching supplements obsessively. Every night, reading studies, calling researchers, digging into anything I could find. And I started weight training. I could barely leg press 50 pounds.

Mid 2023

Built the full protocol

23 supplements. Organized by category. Every single one backed by research I read myself. Exercise grew to 1.5 to 2 hours, 5 to 6 days a week. The gym became my second home.

2024

Improvements visible

Ankles stable. Knees stable on stairs. Speech improving. Leg pressing 200 pounds. Up from 50. People who hadn't seen me in months couldn't believe the difference.

2025

Started sharing the protocol

Phone calls with other ALS patients became routine. People started reaching out after hearing how long I'd been going. I joined the Rocky Mountain ALS community. What started as one-on-one calls turned into something bigger.

Mar 2026

This site launches

Everything I know, organized for others. My protocol, my routine, my mindset. So you don't have to piece it together alone.

I'M NOT DYING TODAY.
