L-Serine for ALS: What the Research Says and Why I Take It

If I could only take one supplement for ALS, it would be L-Serine.

L-Serine is a naturally occurring amino acid that your body uses to make proteins, cell membranes, and other amino acids, and it's found in foods like soybeans, nuts, eggs, and meat. It has FDA GRAS status (Generally Recognized as Safe), which means it's considered safe for consumption.

But what caught my attention, and what catches the attention of many ALS patients, is a clinical trial that showed remarkable results.

The Research

In a Phase 1 clinical trial (NCT01835782), ALS patients who took 30 grams of L-Serine per day showed roughly 85% slower functional decline compared to historical controls. The trial was small and Phase 1 trials are primarily designed to test safety rather than efficacy, but the results were striking enough to get real attention in the ALS community.

The research originated from Dr. Paul Cox, an ethnobotanist who studied the unusually high rates of ALS-like disease in Guam and traced it to a neurotoxin called BMAA found in cycad seeds that were part of the local diet. L-Serine appeared to compete with BMAA, potentially blocking its toxic effects.

How I Take It

I take 30 grams of L-Serine per day, split into two doses:

  • 15 grams in the morning, mixed into a drink
  • 15 grams in the evening, mixed into a drink

It dissolves reasonably well in water and has a slightly sweet taste, and I have taken it consistently for years now.

Important Notes

I need to be clear about a few things:

  • The Phase 1 trial was small and primarily measured safety, not efficacy. Larger trials are needed.
  • L-Serine is an amino acid, not a drug. It is not FDA-approved as a treatment for ALS.
  • My neurologist, Dr. Richard Bedlack at Duke, is aware that I take L-Serine and supports it. Always consult your physician.
  • 30 grams per day is a high dose. Start with a conversation with your doctor.
  • This is one piece of the puzzle. L-Serine is one of roughly 20 supplements I take daily. No single supplement is a silver bullet. If you want the complete picture, check out the full protocol.

I have been taking L-Serine every single day for years, and honestly I cannot isolate the effect of any one supplement in my stack. What I can tell you is that I am still here, still walking, still fighting, and that is enough for me to keep going.
