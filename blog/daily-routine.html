My Daily Routine with ALS: Supplements, Exercise, and Structure

People often ask me what my day looks like. When you are taking roughly 20 supplements, staying active, and managing ALS, structure matters. Here is a general overview of how I organize my day.

My Morning Routine

My morning starts with my first round of supplements. This includes my vitamins, my first dose of L-Serine (15g mixed into a drink), and several of my muscle-support supplements.

I take certain supplements with food and others on an empty stomach, depending on the supplement. The full protocol includes the specific timing for each one.

Midday: Movement and Supplements

I stay active. Exercise looks different for every ALS patient depending on their progression, but I believe in moving as much as I can, as often as I can. My exercise routine has evolved over the years as my capabilities have changed, but the commitment to daily movement has not.

I take my afternoon supplements around lunch, including HMB and creatine for muscle support.

My Evening Wind-Down

My evening routine includes my second dose of L-Serine (15g), my remaining supplements, and wind-down activities. Consistency matters, I have taken my supplements at roughly the same times every day for years.

The Mindset Piece

I will be the first to tell you that supplements are only part of the equation. My mindset, how I think about ALS, how I approach each day, what keeps me fighting, is something I consider just as important as anything in a pill bottle.

The full protocol includes a dedicated section on my mindset framework. It is not self-help platitudes. It is how a real ALS patient who has been living with this disease for more than six years actually thinks about it, day to day.

Why Routine Matters

ALS is unpredictable. Some days are better than others. Having a routine means that even on hard days, the supplements get taken, the body gets moved, and the mind stays focused. I credit my consistency as one of the most important factors in my journey.
