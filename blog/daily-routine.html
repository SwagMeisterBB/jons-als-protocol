What Jon's Day Looks Like: Supplements, Exercise, and Routine

By Jon Edy • March 2026 • 4 min read

People often ask Jon what his day looks like. When you're taking ~20 supplements, staying active, and managing ALS, structure matters. Here's a general overview of how Jon organizes his day.

Morning

Jon's morning starts with his first round of supplements. This includes his vitamins, his first dose of L-Serine (15g mixed into a drink), and several of his muscle-support supplements.

He takes certain supplements with food and others on an empty stomach, depending on the supplement. The full protocol includes the specific timing for each one.

Midday

Jon stays active. Exercise looks different for every ALS patient depending on their progression, but Jon believes in moving as much as he can, as often as he can. His exercise routine has evolved over the years as his capabilities have changed, but the commitment to daily movement hasn't.

He takes his afternoon supplements around lunch, including HMB and creatine for muscle support.

Evening

Jon's evening routine includes his second dose of L-Serine (15g), his remaining supplements, and wind-down activities. Consistency matters - Jon has taken his supplements at roughly the same times every day for years.

The Mindset Piece

Jon will be the first to tell you that supplements are only part of the equation. His mindset - how he thinks about ALS, how he approaches each day, what keeps him fighting - is something he considers just as important as anything in a pill bottle.

The full protocol includes a dedicated section on Jon's mindset framework. It's not self-help platitudes. It's how a real ALS patient who's been living with this disease for 6+ years actually thinks about it, day to day.

Why Routine Matters

ALS is unpredictable. Some days are better than others. Having a routine means that even on hard days, the supplements get taken, the body gets moved, and the mind stays focused. Jon credits his consistency as one of the most important factors in his journey.
