HMB for Muscle Preservation: A Bodybuilding Supplement for ALS?

By Jon Edy • March 2026 • 4 min read

HMB (Beta-Hydroxy Beta-Methylbutyrate) is a supplement most people associate with bodybuilding. It's a metabolite of leucine, an essential amino acid, and it's been studied extensively for its ability to reduce muscle protein breakdown.

For ALS patients, muscle breakdown is the core problem. Motor neurons die, muscles stop receiving signals, and they atrophy. Anything that can slow that breakdown is worth investigating.

Why Jon Takes HMB

The logic is straightforward: if HMB reduces muscle breakdown in healthy athletes, it may help preserve muscle in ALS patients whose muscles are wasting due to denervation.

There is limited ALS-specific clinical data on HMB. Most of the research comes from the sports nutrition and geriatric medicine worlds, where HMB has been shown to:

  • Reduce muscle protein breakdown
  • Support lean muscle mass in older adults
  • Help preserve strength during periods of inactivity or bed rest

Jon considers HMB one of his "muscle support" supplements - not a magic bullet, but a reasonable addition to his protocol based on the available research.

How Jon Takes It

Jon takes 3 grams of HMB per day, which is the dose most commonly used in research studies. He takes it in capsule form, split across the day with meals.

Important Notes
